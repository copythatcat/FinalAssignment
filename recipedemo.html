Teriyaki Salmon Bowl

Savor our Teriyaki Salmon Bowl: Grilled salmon glazed in teriyaki perfection, served on rice with corn, boiled egg, and edamame. A delicious, wholesome dish that comes together in less than 30 minutes.

By Jessica Chen

Ingredients

  • 8oz of Salmon
  • 1 Can of Canned Corn
  • 2 Eggs
  • Edamame Beans
  • 1/2 a Roma Tomato
  • 1/2 of a Cucumber
  • 4oz of Red Cabbage
  • About 4 Lettuce Leafs

Step 1. Season fresh salmon fillets with salt, pepper, and a dash of lemon juice. Pre-heat a large pan and drizzle with oil that has a high smoke-point. Pan-sear on medium high until the edges are golden, and the center is flaky, about 4 minutes each side. Though you may be tempted to lift up the salmon, try your best to let it sear completely to achieve a crunchy skin.

Step 2. In the meantime, set a pot of water and bring to a rolling boil. Gently place two eggs in boiling water. Cook for exactly 6 minutes for a perfect soft boil. Transfer to an ice bath to cool, then peel and set aside.

Step 3. Once the Salmon is finished cooking, set aside on a plate to rest. Grab your red cabbage and cucumber and thinly slice them. Set them aside.

Step 4. Arrange the veggie medley at the base of the bowl. Take your salmon and slice into 2 inch chunks. Place a perfectly grilled salmon fillet on top. Take your two soft boiled eggs and slice them in quarters. Delicately nestle the soft-boiled eggs alongside.

Step 5. Finish by drizzling a zesty dressing over the bowl. Choose a light lemon vinaigrette or a creamy dill sauce.
